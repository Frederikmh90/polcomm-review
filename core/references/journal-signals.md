# Journal Signals

The manifest is the source of truth for exact policy claims and verification dates. This file translates those facts into review behavior.

## top-field

When no journal is specified, use a leading communication-field standard without enforcing outlet-specific word caps or repository rules.

## PoliticalCommunication

- Penalize weak linkage between media processes and political significance.
- Raise the bar on campaign, public-opinion, and democratic-process framing.
- Keep formal policy claims conservative because the publicly accessible Taylor & Francis surface is thinner than the Oxford and SAGE entries.

## JOC

- Expect broad communication relevance and clean framing for a wide communication readership.
- Remember the verified 35-page main-document cap and the 150-word abstract limit.
- Penalize narrow subfield framing unless the broader payoff is explicit.

## InformationCommunicationSociety

- Reward work on digitally mediated communication, infrastructure, and platformed social life.
- Be explicit about platform access constraints, API changes, and data provenance.
- Keep journal-specific policy claims conservative because the publicly accessible Taylor & Francis surface is limited.

## CommunicationResearch

- Expect strong empirical execution, clear construct validity, and tight design-to-claim fit.
- Penalize overclaiming from thin measures or weak causal language.
- Reward theory-led empirical papers with clear methodological discipline.

## JCMC

- Reward online communication and computational communication work with clear substantive stakes.
- Remember the verified under-10,000-word cap, 250-word abstract limit, and Data Availability Statement requirement.
- Penalize methods display without communication payoff.

## IJPP

- Raise the bar on media-politics relevance, press systems, campaign news, and public-affairs significance.
- Remember the verified 250-word abstract cap and front-cover-sheet word-count requirement.
- Avoid inventing a journal-wide word cap that the accessible official page does not state.

## DigitalJournalism

- Reward newsroom, platform, and digital-production relevance.
- The public journal page verifies anonymised refereeing and article-type word ranges, but not a clear abstract cap.
- Require explicit discussion of platform dependency and changing technical affordances.

## JournalismStudies

- Reward strong engagement with journalism theory, norms, routines, and professional practice.
- Penalize papers that frame journalism only as background context.
- Keep journal-specific policy claims conservative unless stronger author instructions are verified.

## NewMediaSociety

- Reward platformed-publics, technology-society, and networked communication framing.
- Remember the verified strict anonymous review model, 8,000-word target, and 100 to 150 word abstract requirement.
- Expect explicit treatment of data access, ethics, and digital trace limitations.
