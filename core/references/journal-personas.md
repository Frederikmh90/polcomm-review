# Journal Personas

Read this alongside [`../journal-manifest.json`](../journal-manifest.json).

## top-field

- contribution threshold: clear and credible contribution to a leading communication audience
- theory expectations: theory should discipline the claim and explain the communicative process at stake
- empirical expectations: evidence should match the stated inferential target
- methods expectations: methods should be appropriate, legible, and honestly bounded
- common desk-reject reasons: unclear payoff, weak media or communication relevance, inflated claims, design mismatch

## PoliticalCommunication

- contribution threshold: substantial contribution to political communication or media-and-democracy debates
- theory expectations: connect message, media-system, or public-opinion mechanisms to political stakes
- empirical expectations: show why the evidence supports claims about communication effects, processes, or institutions
- methods expectations: any method is acceptable if it advances a real political-communication question
- common desk-reject reasons: generic media paper with no politics payoff, overclaimed causal language, thin democratic relevance

## JOC

- contribution threshold: broad communication significance
- theory expectations: clear theoretical framing with cross-subfield interest
- empirical expectations: design should be legible to non-specialists in the specific niche
- methods expectations: strong execution in service of a broad communication argument
- common desk-reject reasons: narrow niche framing, weak broader payoff, unclear theory-to-evidence link

## InformationCommunicationSociety

- contribution threshold: clear relevance to digitally mediated communication and social life
- theory expectations: link platform, infrastructure, or mediation concepts to observable communication processes
- empirical expectations: evidence should support the social and communicative claim, not just describe a dataset
- methods expectations: computational, qualitative, or mixed methods are fine if platform limits are handled honestly
- common desk-reject reasons: dataset-driven paper with no conceptual contribution, platform determinism, weak provenance discussion

## CommunicationResearch

- contribution threshold: theory-led empirical contribution with durable communication value
- theory expectations: explicit and testable theoretical payoff
- empirical expectations: high bar for validity, measurement, and design alignment
- methods expectations: rigorous execution matters; weak operationalization is costly
- common desk-reject reasons: construct slippage, vague hypotheses, inflated inference, weak measure quality

## JCMC

- contribution threshold: substantial contribution to computer-mediated communication research
- theory expectations: theory should explain mediated interaction or digital communication processes
- empirical expectations: evidence should address online communication dynamics rather than treating platforms as a convenience sample
- methods expectations: technical sophistication is rewarded only when it clarifies the communication question
- common desk-reject reasons: methods-first paper with no communication payoff, unbounded claims from platform data, weak reproducibility posture

## IJPP

- contribution threshold: meaningful press-politics contribution
- theory expectations: connect media institutions, news production, or political information flows to political outcomes or debates
- empirical expectations: evidence should support claims about journalism-politics relationships and not just media content in isolation
- methods expectations: qualitative, quantitative, and comparative work all fit if the press-politics stakes are clear
- common desk-reject reasons: politics-lite framing, descriptive content paper with no press-politics contribution, poor institutional grounding

## DigitalJournalism

- contribution threshold: meaningful contribution to digital journalism scholarship
- theory expectations: theory should speak to newsroom practice, journalistic production, or digital news distribution
- empirical expectations: evidence should reveal something about journalism, not merely social platforms generally
- methods expectations: reward fit-for-purpose digital methods and clear limits discussion
- common desk-reject reasons: platform paper with no journalism object, ephemeral-case overclaiming, missing discussion of technical change

## JournalismStudies

- contribution threshold: clear journalism-studies contribution
- theory expectations: concepts around roles, routines, norms, publics, and professional identity should be explicit
- empirical expectations: evidence should support claims about journalism as a practice or institution
- methods expectations: mixed traditions are welcome if the concept work is tight
- common desk-reject reasons: treating journalism as a background case, thin norm conceptions, weak professional-context grounding

## NewMediaSociety

- contribution threshold: strong contribution on communication technology and society
- theory expectations: connect technological mediation to social, political, or cultural consequences without deterministic overreach
- empirical expectations: evidence should support claims about platformed publics, participation, or digital life
- methods expectations: digital-trace and computational work must handle validity and ethics explicitly
- common desk-reject reasons: vague big-tech framing, convenience-sample overclaiming, weak ethics or provenance discussion
