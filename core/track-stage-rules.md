# Track and Stage Rules

## Stage Detection

Use explicit manuscript cues first.

- `proposal`: proposal language, planned analyses, expected contribution, dissertation prospectus framing
- `dissertation`: chapter or paper-based dissertation framing, committee or defense language
- `article`: completed manuscript framing, delivered findings, standard article structure

## Stage Gating

### Article

- Expect completed evidence, coherent claims, and audit-ready tables or figures.
- Missing core evidence can be `critical`.

### Proposal

- Do not punish the paper for lacking final estimates or polished tables.
- Focus on whether promised evidence, design logic, measurement strategy, and scope conditions are credible.
- Flag missing final results only when the text incorrectly implies that the evidence already exists.

### Dissertation

- Allow chapter-level heterogeneity and future-work language.
- Evaluate coherence across papers or chapters, contribution integration, and whether the committee-facing framing is honest about readiness.

## Track Detection

Use explicit cues when possible:

- `methods`: estimands, validation studies, benchmarking, annotation reliability methods, simulation, inferential workflow contribution
- `quant`: surveys, experiments, panels, content-analysis models, observational media-effects designs, tables, figures
- `formal`: propositions, equilibrium, proofs, comparative statics
- `qual`: interviews, archives, discourse analysis, rhetorical analysis, ethnography, process tracing
- `mixed`: substantial integration across qualitative and quantitative evidence

## Track Branching

### Methods

- Raise the bar in Module 7 for notation, validation logic, benchmarking, robustness, and generality claims.

### Quant

- Focus in Modules 5, 6, and 7 on measurement, identification discipline, uncertainty, and design-to-claim fit.

### Formal

- Focus in Modules 4 and 7 on assumptions, internal logic, proposition sequencing, and interpretation of the model.

### Qual

- Focus in Modules 4, 5, 6, and 7 on concept formation, case logic, evidentiary chain, and alternative explanations.

### Mixed

- Check in Module 7 whether the components genuinely speak to the same inferential claim rather than sitting side by side.
